0 · The reframe (the single most important decision)

PS4 literally asks for: occlusion-robust road extraction → graph healing → criticality (Gatekeeper Nodes) → stress testing → dashboard. That is the baseline ~80% of teams will build. We do not change the problem statement — we change its altitude.

From: "Detect roads from satellite images, even under trees/clouds."   To: "Predict how a city's mobility network fails under a disaster — before it happens — and tell emergency responders exactly which junction, bridge or road to protect, and how to reroute when it breaks."

GARUDA turns multi-source satellite imagery (including ISRO's new NISAR radar) into an uncertainty-aware, routable infrastructure graph, fuses it with structural-risk and hazard layers, runs failure simulations, and delivers NDEM-style decision support. Every road we extract exists to answer one question: "if this fails, who gets cut off, and what do we do?"

Why this framing wins (grounded in our research)

The idea phase cut ~99.7% of teams in 2025 (8,744 teams → 30 finalists). Judges are NRSC scientists whose day job is turning satellite data into disaster decision-support (NDEM). A road map impresses no one in that room; a decision-support resilience platform built on their newest satellite (NISAR) and their operational paradigm (NDEM) speaks their language. It is faithful to PS4, but operates at the level ISRO would actually deploy.

0★ · The one thing to remember (read this first)

The human story. In the 2015 Chennai floods, 100+ patients — many on ventilators — were trapped inside Global Hospital, cut off from the city for 48 hours and finally evacuated by fishing boats; at MIOT Hospital, 14 patients died when isolation cut their power and oxygen. Responders could see where the water was. What no one could see in time was which single road and bridge failures had severed those hospitals from the city. GARUDA exists to see that — before it becomes a tragedy.

The one line

"GARUDA turns India's satellites into a living resilience intelligence system — predicting which single failure will cut a hospital off from the people who need it, and acting before the flood arrives."

★ GARUDA's Universal Mission: "No Indian community should become digitally invisible — before, during, or after a disaster."

Roads. Hospitals. Power. Water. Telecom. Schools. Relief. Every consequence flows from this one mission. GARUDA begins with urban road resilience and scales into India's National Infrastructure Intelligence Platform. The mission is not "detect roads" — it is guaranteed digital visibility for every Indian community in crisis.

★ The signature: the Zero-Isolation Engine

If a judge forgets everything else, they remember this. GARUDA's mission is Zero Isolation — no hospital, shelter or community in India should ever be cut off by a single, predictable failure. The Zero-Isolation Engine continuously finds every single-point-of-failure whose loss isolates a critical facility, and recommends the minimum-cost reinforcement that guarantees a backup route. Memorable, measurable, and the soul of the project.

The hero demo — one story, start to finish

Pre-monsoon rain builds → NISAR InSAR flags a weakening flyover embankment → GARUDA's living twin simulates the coming flood → the Zero-Isolation Engine detects this one failure isolates City Hospital from ~150,000 residents → Guardian Mode pushes an alert 36 hours early → it recommends reinforcing one bridge and opening an evacuation corridor → officials act → the hospital stays connected. That is the demo.

City Pulse — the number people remember

GARUDA outputs one unforgettable figure per city — the City Pulse, a live Resilience Score (0–100) — and headline statements like "this single flyover failure strands ~150k people and 1 hospital; reinforcing one bridge restores ~73% of cut-off access." Numbers are produced live by the simulation on the pilot city — illustrative here, never assumed.

★ Time-to-Isolation (TTI) — the decision metric no one else has

Alongside the City Pulse score, GARUDA introduces a new operational metric: Time-to-Isolation (TTI) — how many hours until a critical facility becomes unreachable if current infrastructure conditions continue deteriorating. Unlike a 0–100 score, TTI is immediately actionable. A score of 72/100 tells a planner "things are bad." TTI = 9 hours tells them what to do in the next hour.

3 · The end-to-end architecture (master view)

Eight working layers from raw satellite data to a control-room decision, plus one roadmap layer. purple = where ISRO's NISAR/SAR does the heavy lifting

L0
Multi-source EO ingestion & harmonization — optical (Cartosat-3, LISS-IV, Sentinel-2) + SAR (Sentinel-1, NISAR) + DEM + OSM + IMD rainfall. Co-register, tile, speckle-filter, normalize. Multi-temporal stacking for Infrastructure Memory baseline.
▼
L1
Occlusion-robust road extraction — transformer segmentation (SegFormer/Mask2Former, ViT) with a dual-stream optical+SAR fusion encoder. Recovers roads under cloud/canopy/shadow.
▼
L2
Uncertainty & occlusion intelligence — per-pixel road confidence + missing-segment/occlusion probability (evidential DL / MC-dropout / ensemble). Certainty, not hallucination.
▼
L3
Topological healing → routable graph — skeletonize → nodes/edges; MST + Disjoint-Set gap-bridging weighted by confidence + geometric priors. Output: connected weighted graph (cost + confidence + structural risk).
▼
L4
Structural-risk fusion (NISAR InSAR) + Infrastructure Memory — cm-level deformation → subsidence/bridge/embankment risk; SAR flood-water mapping; DEM landslide susceptibility → per-edge/node vulnerability scores. Multi-pass time-series builds Infrastructure Memory: what has changed over 6 months, 1 year, 5 years.
▼
L5
Criticality, accessibility & TTI engine — betweenness centrality + articulation points → Gatekeeper Nodes; facility accessibility (hospitals/shelters via OSM) → "who is isolated if X fails." TTI computation: deformation trend rate + hazard model + graph accessibility → hours-to-isolation per critical facility.
▼
L6
Resilience digital twin, scenario engine & Recovery Intelligence — overlay hazard → ablate nodes/edges → recompute global network efficiency → Resilience Index; emergency reroutes, evacuation corridors, priority-repair, lead-time alerts. Investment Optimization: greedy budget allocation for max resilience gain per ₹. Recovery mode: post-event SAR damage mapping → repair sequence → reconstruction tracking.
▼
L7
Decision-support dashboard (NDEM-aligned) + Mission Control — criticality heatmap, TTI alerts, scenario toggles, Next Failure panel, recommended actions, impact report; export to NDEM/Bhuvan paradigm. Explainability layer: every ranked item has a plain-English "why."
▼
L8
(Roadmap, not core) on-prem / air-gapped LLM planning copilot — natural-language "what-if" queries + auto incident reports. Shown as future vision.

Two flows in one (this distinction wins the Process-flow + Architecture slides)

Build-time pipeline (L0→L3): heavy ML, run offline / pre-finale on archived data. Run-time decision loop (L4→L7): lightweight graph + simulation, runs live in the control room on CPU. Separating these proves we know what's expensive vs what's interactive.

3★ · GARUDA is a closed-loop Resilience OS — not a linear pipeline

A sophisticated pipeline (data → AI → graph → dashboard) is still passive and linear. The leap scientist-judges reward is an adaptive, self-improving operating system for infrastructure resilience. Positioning matters: Windows is an OS, not an app — GARUDA is India's Infrastructure Resilience OS. The city's road network is its first organ; the OS is built to host more (see §12★).

The OS loop: GARUDA runs a continuous SENSE → UNDERSTAND → FORESEE → ACT → LEARN cycle that feeds back on itself — it does not end at a dashboard.
SENSE · L0→ UNDERSTAND · L1–L4→ FORESEE · L5–L6→ ACT · L7 + ecosystem→ LEARN⟲ back to SENSE

The three feedback loops that make it adaptive (scientists love this)

1 · Active re-acquisition — the system decides what to look at next. When the scenario engine finds a high-impact but low-confidence failure point, it requests targeted re-evaluation of that area → uncertainty drops → graph & risk scores update → the simulation re-runs. GARUDA actively closes its own blind spots. Roadmap · PoC: one manual re-evaluation trigger
2 · Living Digital Twin — continuous data assimilation. The twin is not a snapshot. It continuously ingests new satellite passes, NISAR InSAR deformation, IMD rainfall/forecast and traffic, and auto-updates its state (the same data-assimilation principle behind numerical weather models). Criticality, TTI, and the Resilience Index recompute as reality changes. PoC* · lightweight: 1–2 feeds refresh the twin
3 · Human-in-the-loop & learn-from-outcomes. Planners confirm/reject healed roads and actions → priors improve. After a real event, the twin checks whether its prediction held (TTI accuracy, isolation predicted vs. observed) and updates — so GARUDA gets smarter with every disaster. Roadmap

Passive dashboard → proactive intelligence

A passive system waits for a click. GARUDA's Proactive Risk Sentinel runs always-on, continuously scanning plausible hazards and pushing the top risks before anyone asks: "City Hospital isolates if the Hebbal flyover floods — TTI = 11 hours — pre-position boats in Ward 7 now." That is intelligence, not visualization. PoC

GIS map → Mission Control (our product strength)

The UI is a resilience mission-control room, not a layer-toggle map: Mission Status · Threat Level · Infrastructure Health Index · TTI Dashboard · Recommended Actions · Simulation Timeline · Resource Allocation. It should feel like an ISRO control room. PoC core panels

5 · Component deep dive — what · why · how · consequences

For each layer: exactly what it does, why it must exist, how we build it, what it unlocks (and what breaks without it), and how it ties to ISRO's real work.

LAYER 0

Multi-source EO ingestion & harmonization

What Pull and align all inputs into analysis-ready tiles: optical (Cartosat-3, Resourcesat LISS-IV, Sentinel-2), SAR (Sentinel-1, NISAR), DEM (CartoDEM/SRTM), OSM vectors+POIs, IMD rainfall. Also stack multi-temporal SAR acquisitions for Infrastructure Memory baseline.
Why Roads must be observable in every condition; a single sensor is blind under cloud/canopy. Every downstream layer assumes pixel-aligned, calibrated data — get this wrong and fusion produces noise.
How Sources: Bhoonidhi (ISRO), Copernicus, NOAA/AWS. Optical–SAR co-registration (ESA SNAP / GDAL / OTB); SAR speckle filtering (Refined Lee), radiometric + terrain correction; tile to 512²/1024²; normalize. Pretraining pairs from SEN1-2 / OpenSatMap. Multi-temporal SAR stack: SBAS-InSAR or PS-InSAR processing for deformation time-series.
Consequences The silent foundation — good harmonization makes fusion trivial; bad alignment poisons everything. Tradeoff: SAR–optical co-registration is fiddly → we lean on pre-aligned public datasets for the PoC.
ISRO tie-in Runs on Bhoonidhi (44-satellite archive) + the NISAR portal; indigenous Cartosat/Resourcesat → an Atmanirbhar, India-data stack.
LAYER 1

Occlusion-robust road extraction (optical–SAR fusion)

What A deep segmentation model that outputs road probability from fused optical + SAR input.
Why This is PS4's central pain — "spectral blindness." Trees, shadows and cloud create topological breaks; single-sensor extractors provably fail under occlusion. Radar penetrates cloud and canopy, so SAR supplies structure exactly where optical dies.
How Transformer segmentation (SegFormer / Mask2Former, ViT backbone) with a dual-stream encoder (optical + SAR) fused via cross-attention / discrepancy-conditioned fusion. Loss = Dice + IoU + boundary-aware + optional connectivity loss; train with simulated occlusions; pretrain on SpaceNet + DeepGlobe, fine-tune on the pilot city.
Consequences Big jump in occlusion-recall (the metric PS4 stresses) → continuous roads where baseline teams get gaps. Tradeoff: two streams cost more compute/data → bounded by PoC scope and pretrained backbones.
ISRO tie-in ISRO already runs AI-on-EO (deforestation reporting 1 yr→1 mo; Cartosat-3 water-body DNN at 96%). SAR–optical fusion is peer-reviewed (GLF-CR, D3FNet). Uses NISAR's all-weather strength as designed.
LAYER 2

Uncertainty & occlusion intelligence

What Per-pixel road confidence + occlusion / missing-segment probability alongside the mask.
Why A confident-but-wrong road can send an ambulance into a dead end. Planners must see where the model is guessing. Uncertainty also drives smarter healing (L3) and an honest UI (L7).
How Evidential deep learning (Dirichlet) or MC-dropout / deep ensembles → confidence map; temperature-scaling calibration; measure with Expected Calibration Error. Low-confidence / occluded segments are flagged distinctly, never silently filled.
Consequences Enables confidence-weighted healing and trustworthy decisions; calibration is quantifiable. Without it the platform silently lies — disqualifying for safety-of-life use.
ISRO tie-in Mirrors ISRO's heightened reliability/quality-assurance focus after the EOS-09 (18 May 2025) failure — responsible AI for governance.
LAYER 3

Topological healing → routable graph

What Convert the pixel mask into a connected, weighted, routable vector graph.
Why PS4 is explicit: fragmented masks can't compute shortest paths, detours or failures. Topology is the bridge from "image" to "network."
How Morphological skeletonization → 1-px centerlines → nodes (intersections/endpoints) + edges. MST + Disjoint-Set bridge gaps using Euclidean distance + angular alignment + L2 confidence. Edge weight = travel cost (length/speed-limit); edge attribute = structural confidence.
Consequences Produces the object every downstream layer needs; PS4's connectivity-ratio metric jumps after healing. Confidence-gating stops us "healing" fake roads.
ISRO tie-in Implements PS4's prescribed MST/Disjoint-Set; yields an NNRMS-grade routable road asset.
LAYER 4 · the novel, sensor-correct layer + Infrastructure Memory

Structural-risk fusion (NISAR InSAR) & Infrastructure Memory

What Enrich every edge/node with a structural-vulnerability score and hazard-exposure score. Build an Infrastructure Memory from multi-pass InSAR time-series — what has changed over months and years.
Why A road that exists is not a road that's safe. Bridges deform measurably for months before they fail; floods and landslides disable specific segments. ISRO's greatest unique strength is repeated Earth observation — no ground-based system can match NISAR's 12-day repeat, 4 mm/year sensitivity. A static snapshot wastes this advantage; Infrastructure Memory exploits it.
How NISAR / Sentinel-1 InSAR time-series (SBAS/PS-InSAR) → cm-level deformation trends (bridge/embankment/road subsidence motion); SAR all-weather flood mapping; DEM slope + flow → landslide susceptibility. Infrastructure Memory: build deformation velocity maps + anomaly detection (is this bridge sinking faster than it was 6 months ago?). Peer-reviewed 2025 research confirms InSAR clustering algorithms detect bridge displacement trends at mm accuracy.
Consequences Converts the graph into a condition-aware, time-aware twin → failure scenarios become evidence-based predictions, not random stress tests. The TTI engine pulls directly from Infrastructure Memory (deformation velocity → time-to-failure estimate). Tradeoff: InSAR time-series is heavy → PoC uses archived/derived data; live ingestion is roadmap.
ISRO tie-in NISAR's headline applications are deformation + infrastructure + disaster; echoes RISAT flood mapping (Wayanad, Jul 2024) and NDEM's Hazard–Vulnerability–Risk workflow. NISAR's 12-day repeat is uniquely suited to temporal infrastructure monitoring that no private or government data stream can replicate — the satellite-first advantage.
LAYER 5

Criticality, accessibility & TTI engine

What Rank nodes/edges by systemic importance, compute facility access, and calculate Time-to-Isolation (TTI) per critical facility.
Why Planners need the single points of failure, the answer to "who loses hospital/shelter access if this breaks?" — AND how long they have to act.
How Betweenness centrality → Gatekeeper Nodes; articulation-point / cut-vertex detection; shortest-path accessibility from population grids to hospitals/shelters. TTI engine: for each critical facility, compute: (current edge vulnerability + deformation velocity from L4) / (accessibility loss rate under projected hazard) → hours until isolation. Combines Infrastructure Memory + hazard forecast + graph dynamics into one actionable number.
Consequences Produces the criticality heatmap + facility-isolation + TTI dashboard (PS4 + beyond). TTI is a new operational metric ISRO scientists haven't seen presented this way — that's the differentiation. Targeted (centrality-based) ranking is provably more disruptive than random → it focuses scarce attention where it matters.
ISRO tie-in Directly delivers PS4's "Gatekeeper Nodes"; TTI supports NDEM situational assessment and AMRUT urban-infrastructure planning. OECD/ITF 2025 transport resilience policy cites rapidity (time to failure/recovery) as a core resilience dimension — TTI operationalizes this.
LAYER 6 · the demo that wins the room

Resilience digital twin, scenario engine & Investment Optimization

What Simulate disasters/failures on the twin, quantify resilience, generate actions, and optimize infrastructure investments.
Why PS4's stress-testing — and the pre-emptive capability ISRO's NDEM is weakest on. "Predict how the city fails before it fails." Also: government asks "we have ₹50 crore — where do we spend it?" GARUDA should answer that.
How Hazard overlay → disable affected nodes/edges (percolation framework) → recompute global efficiency, largest connected component, facility accessibility → Resilience Index = perturbed ÷ baseline efficiency. Generate: emergency reroutes, evacuation corridors, priority-repair ranking. Investment Optimization: greedy/combinatorial search — "if we strengthen these 4 bridges, overall city resilience increases 18%." Budget-constrained network robustness optimization (peer-reviewed: resilience-based component importance metrics + budget allocation, 2024).
Consequences The interactive, unforgettable demo: click "flood" → city reorganizes, hospital lights up as isolated, reroute appears. Investment Optimization turns GARUDA from a diagnostic tool into a capital allocation advisor for NDMA/municipalities — a genuinely different value proposition. Tradeoff: full combinatorial optimization is NP-hard → use greedy top-k bridge reinforcement for PoC.
ISRO tie-in Extends NDEM's HVR + decision-support with the pre-emptive layer it lacks; disaster resilience is the host center's (NRSC) core mandate. Investment optimization is precisely what AMRUT cities (500 cities, GIS master plans) need — a satellite-derived evidence base for infrastructure spend.
LAYER 7

Decision-support dashboard (NDEM-aligned) + Explainability

What The control-room interface that delivers decisions, not just maps — with a full Explainability layer so every ranked item comes with a reason.

Government officials will not act on a black-box ranking — they need to understand why before they authorize action. XAI research (2024–25) confirms that feature attribution and input-output visualization are essential for trust in emergency management AI.

Every TTI score, every ranked action, every investment recommendation comes with a plain-English explanation.
Consequences Makes GARUDA feel like deployable government software, not an experiment. Explainability builds the trust that moves officials from "interesting demo" to "we want to use this." The layer your team is strongest at and where decks win or lose.
ISRO tie-in Mirrors Bhuvan NextGen "Disaster mode" and NDEM DSS → framed as their next-generation tool. Prototype-based Explainable Neural Networks for geospatial learning tasks (2026 research) confirms XAI is the frontier for EO decision support.

12★ · The 2028 question — and GARUDA's North Star

The discipline almost no team practices: before designing, ask one question — "If NRSC adopted GARUDA in 2028, what would it still be missing?" — and build toward that answer. That is the line between a hackathon demo and real research.

The honest answer

A road-resilience OS, however good, still treats roads in isolation. Real disasters cascade across coupled infrastructures — roads ↔ power ↔ water ↔ telecom ↔ health. A flooded substation kills the pump that drains the road that reaches the hospital. The research literature is blunt: modelling these interdependencies is "indispensable yet often not even attempted," and low-probability, high-consequence cascading power–water–mobility failures are the real risk.

Also still missing: learn-from-outcomes (did the recommended action actually work?), equity-aware prioritization (who physically cannot evacuate), and upstream hazard forecasting (couple with IMD numerical forecasts instead of only reacting to InSAR/rainfall).

GARUDA's North Star: evolve from a road-resilience OS into a multi-infrastructure resilience OS — roads first (the most satellite-observable layer, and our PoC), then power, water, telecom and health as additional graph layers, modelling the cross-network cascade. Every infrastructure layer feeds the universal mission: no Indian community becomes digitally invisible.

★ Recovery Intelligence — closing the full disaster lifecycle

Current GARUDA covers: Predict → Respond → Reroute. One critical phase is missing from the current architecture: Recovery. The same satellite-first advantage that predicts failure can power recovery intelligence after the event:

Disaster ends
↓
NISAR / SAR re-pass → damage extent mapped automatically
↓
Repair priority ranked by population impact + structural severity
↓
Recovery time estimated per corridor
↓
Reconstruction monitored — satellite detects when repair is complete
↓
Before vs. After comparison validates the intervention
↓
GARUDA learns: did predicted TTI match observed isolation? Update the model.

This closes GARUDA's loop across the entire disaster lifecycle — before (prediction + TTI), during (response + routing), and after (recovery intelligence). Fulfilling the universal mission: no community stays digitally invisible, even in the aftermath. Peer-reviewed research (2024–25) confirms satellite imagery + AI is the fastest and most scalable post-disaster damage assessment approach, with AI models trained on satellite imagery now generating rapid infrastructure damage reports.

Roadmap — explicitly beyond the PoC · PoC: one historical before/after comparison (Chennai 2015 or Wayanad 2024) as validation proof

Why ISRO — specifically — should build this (the uniquely-satellite argument)

The field's #1 blocker is data-sharing: power/water/telecom data is locked inside departments and rarely shared. Satellite observation sidesteps that entirely — ISRO can observe infrastructure condition from orbit (flooded substations, subsidence over buried water mains, damaged corridors) without needing any department's private data. A satellite-first resilience OS — with Infrastructure Memory, TTI, and Recovery Intelligence — is therefore uniquely positioned to crack the exact problem the field is stuck on. That is GARUDA's research frontier — and the reason it belongs at ISRO, not a generic tech company. Roadmap — explicitly beyond the PoC
