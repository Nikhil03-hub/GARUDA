PS4 — Route Resilience: Occlusion-Robust Road Extraction & Graph-Theoretic Criticality

Data: open & readyKiller dashboard demoBuilt for a 30-hr hackathonStrong, defensible USP

The idea in one line: "See-through-occlusions" road extraction from satellite imagery → heal it into a routable graph → find the city's single points of failure → let a planner click a junction to simulate a flood/closure and instantly see rerouting & travel-time blow-up.

Why it wins the idea phase: the PS itself is engineered for a 30-hr build and hands you a clear novel USP (Resilience Index, "Gatekeeper Nodes", node-ablation what-if).
Why it fits you: CV model + graph logic + an interactive Streamlit/Leaflet dashboard = exactly your full-stack + AI + product wheelhouse (CareerOS-style).
Data readiness (high): SpaceNet, DeepGlobe, OpenSatMap (pretraining), OSM auto-generates ground-truth road masks, Sentinel-2 (10 m) & Resourcesat LISS-IV (5.8 m) open via Bhoonidhi. Cartosat-3 given at finale.
Differentiation: most teams stop at a road mask. You deliver topology + a resilience simulator — a decision-support tool, not a segmentation demo.

Winning angle for slide 3 (USP)

"Existing road-extraction breaks under tree canopy/shadows and outputs disconnected masks useless for routing. We add (1) a transformer/attention model that infers continuity under occlusion, (2) graph-healing (MST + Disjoint-Set) to close gaps along natural trajectories, and (3) a Resilience Index + interactive stress-test no other team will have." Map this to ISRO's NNRMS / Cartosat-Resourcesat downstream-utility mandate.

🥈

PS12 — Fill in the Frames: AI/ML Optical-Flow Frame Interpolation for Satellite Imagery

Data: fully openBest "wow" animation demoPretrained models exist

The idea in one line: turn INSAT's 30-min satellite frames into smooth 15- / 7.5-min "video" by AI-interpolating intermediate frames — sharper near-real-time tracking of cyclones, storms and fires, with a side-by-side time-lapse dashboard.

Why it wins: the demo is a jaw-dropping before/after time-lapse — judges see the value instantly. Clean, objective metrics (SSIM/PSNR/MSE).
Why it fits you: fine-tune a pretrained RIFE/Super-SloMo (fast) + build the web visualisation — light on data wrangling, heavy on the product polish you're good at.
Data readiness (highest): GOES-19 ABI on an open AWS bucket, Himawari open, INSAT-3DS/3DR TIR via MOSDAC. Train on GOES (high cadence), apply to INSAT.
Differentiation: satellite-specific optical flow tuned to cloud motion (vs generic video models that blur fast non-linear cloud dynamics).

Risk to manage

Lowest data risk of the three; main effort is a clean .nc pipeline + a crisp metrics story. Excellent "safe but impressive" choice.

🥉

PS5 — AI-Powered Digital Twin of India's Climate

Data: open, some parsingBiggest vision & narrativeAtmanirbhar / national impact

The idea in one line: a living virtual replica of India's climate that ingests IMD + INSAT data, predicts rainfall/temperature for a pilot region, and lets you run "what-if" scenarios on an interactive map dashboard.

Why it wins: the "Digital Twin + Atmanirbhar Bharat" narrative is the most quotable, mission-aligned story in the whole set — strong with ISRO judges.
Why it fits you: map dashboard + what-if simulator + ML PoC = product-heavy, your strength. Scope to a pilot region keeps it finale-feasible.
Data readiness (good): IMD gridded rainfall/temp are open (binary .grd — needs a parser), INSAT LST/SST/rainfall via MOSDAC (free account). Reliable but budget a day for IMD format handling.
Differentiation: frame it as a true twin (live state + scenario engine), not just a forecast model — that's the gap most teams miss.

Risk to manage

"Digital twin" is broad — judges punish vagueness. We win by scoping hard to one pilot basin + two variables and showing a concrete what-if (e.g., "+2 °C → monsoon onset shift").

My recommendation: PS4 for the highest ceiling (novel, demo-rich, data-ready, perfectly on your skills) — or PS12 if you want the lowest-risk path with the most dazzling demo. PS5 if you want the biggest "national mission" story. Tell me which and I'll build the full 10-slide deck + architecture + dashboard mock.

PS1Optimizing Urban Heat Mitigation & Cooling Strategies via AI/ML

Domain: Geospatial climate ML
Data readiness: High · all open
Data: Landsat-8 & ECOSTRESS LST, Sentinel-2 LULC, ERA5 + CPCB met, OSM/GHSL urban form
Fit: geo + physics-informed ML + dashboard

Goal: a physics-informed geospatial AI system to find urban heat hotspots, quantify drivers (land use, morphology, vegetation, atmosphere), model LST dynamics, and simulate cooling interventions (green/cool roofs, albedo, tree cover, water bodies) with an estimated °C reduction per option.

Outcomes: heat-stress maps, driver attribution, validated AIML model, scenario evaluation, and an optimal intervention plan (type · placement · °C reduction). Optional tools: SOLWEIG, InVEST.
